WHAT IS DIESEL EXHAUST FLUID

Diesel exhaust fluid is used to help break down the hazardous nitrogen oxide (NOx) concentration in emissions from your diesel exhaust stream and turn it into safe water and nitrogen. It is made up of 32.5 percent urea and 67.5 percent deionized water and is a non-hazardous solution. It is consumed in a system called a selective catalytic reduction (SCR).Diesel exhaust fluid does not come in contact with the diesel fuel in your truck and is stored in a completely separate tank.To make sure that the engine reaches full combustion and to prevent exhausting fuel that is not burnt, diesel engines run with a specific air-to-fuel ratio. When there is excess air, the process creates nitrogen oxides. Nitrogen oxides are harmful pollutants. Therefore, the SCR is required to reduce the amount of NOx emissions before it shoots out the exhaust pipe and pollutes the air.How diesel exhaust fluid DEF works within this system is all about chemistry. The diesel exhaust fluid is injected into the exhaust system. Its urea component vaporizes and creates carbon dioxide and ammonia. Ammonia will “catalytically reduce” the amount of nitrogen oxides, turning it into regular nitrogen and water before being released through the exhaust steam. Both these elements are not harmful to the environment.
